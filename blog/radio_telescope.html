Design Spec: Dueling 4m "Stressed Parabola"

Status: CONSTRUCTION PHASE 1
Mission: Low-cost interferometric imaging of the Crab Pulsar.

TARGET ACQUISITION: CRAB PULSAR (B0531+21)
The Crab Pulsar is a neutron star spinning 30 times per second. It is one of the brightest radio sources in the sky (Taurus A).

Goal: Detect the giant radio pulses (GRPs) using a 2-element interferometer to cancel out local RFI (Radio Frequency Interference).

1. The Design: "Stressed Parabola" (Mesh)

A solid 4m dish is too heavy and expensive. We use a geodesic stressed rib design. By bending straight conduit tubes over a central fulcrum, we approximate a parabola within 98% accuracy.

  • Ribs: 3/4" EMT conduit (Electrical Metallic Tubing). It bends elastically to form the curve.
  • Reflector: 1/2" Hardware cloth. At 1420 MHz (21cm wavelength), 1/2" mesh acts like a solid mirror (leakage < 1%).
  • Focal Ratio (f/D): 0.6. This places the focus ~2.4m from the center, easy to support.

2. The Mount: Alt-Azimuth

Equatorial mounts are massive. We use Alt-Azimuth and track the pulsar via software-controlled stepper motors.

  • Azimuth: Re-purposed Truck Wheel Hub (Ford F-150 rear axle). Indestructible and smooth.
  • Altitude: Heavy-duty linear actuator (Solar tracker style) or pillow-blocks.

3. Electronics Chain

To see the Crab, we need extreme sensitivity (+Gain) and low noise (Low K).
