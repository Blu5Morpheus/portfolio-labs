Design Spec: Dueling 4m "Stressed Parabola" Interferometer

Status: PLANNING
Objective: Low-cost, high-performance Hydrogen Line (1420 MHz) interferometry using hardware store materials.

1. The Design: "Stressed Parabola" (Mesh)

A solid 4m dish is too heavy and expensive. We will build a mesh dish (geodesic/stressed rib) to reduce wind load.

  • Ribs: 1/2" or 3/4" EMT conduit. Cheap, bendable, strong.
  • Hub: Central aluminum or plywood plate.
  • Reflector: 1/2" Hardware cloth (galvanized steel mesh). Physics check: 1420MHz = 21cm wavelength. Mesh openings < 2.1cm. 1/2" (~1.27cm) is perfect.

2. The Mount: Alt-Azimuth

Equatorial mounts are too heavy for this scale. We use Alt-Azimuth (Up-Down, Left-Right) and handle tracking in software.

  • Azimuth (Rotation): Junkyard truck rear axle assembly or wheel hub. Bolts to concrete pier.
  • Altitude (Tilt): Linear actuator or heavy-duty pillow block bearings (Dobsonian style).

3. Electronics Chain

A. Offset Feed: Reduces aperture blockage. Use a Biquad or Helical antenna for budget/gain balance.
B. LNA (Low Noise Amplifier): Critical. Place immediately at the feed. Recommended: Nooelec SAWBird+ H1.
C. Coherent Interferometry: The "Dueling" dishes need phase coherence.

  • Option A: KrakenSDR (5-channel coherent radio).
  • Option B: Share a single clock crystal across two RTL-SDRs (Soldering required).
